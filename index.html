Perovskite solar cells fascinate me because they sit at the edge of what's possible — extraordinarily efficient in the lab, yet still fragile enough that the world hasn't fully unlocked them. My research has been a journey through that gap: from simulating nanoparticle interlayers that pushed my thesis model to a 22% power conversion efficiency, to applying machine learning to uncover hidden patterns in multilayer device behaviour, to probing the quantum-level optoelectronic properties of perovskite compounds through first-principles DFT. Alongside this, three years engineering steel in an industrial furnace have taught me something no simulation can — how materials actually behave under real-world pressure, and how far the distance is between a promising result and a manufacturable one. I want to close that distance.

02 — Research

Research Interests

Nanoparticle-Enhanced Perovskite Solar Cells

Investigating how nanoparticle-based interlayers (particularly silver nanoparticles) influence carrier transport, light trapping, and overall power conversion efficiency in perovskite photovoltaic devices.

Machine Learning for Semiconductor Optimization

Applying ML algorithms to simulate and optimize multilayered perovskite cell architectures, identifying non-intuitive parameter relationships that conventional simulation misses.

First-Principles & Computational Materials Design

Using DFT-based first-principles calculations to probe optoelectronic properties of perovskite compounds, bridging atomistic insights with device-level performance modeling.

Microstructural & Optoelectronic Characterization

Correlating microstructural evolution (via SEM-EDS, XRD, optical microscopy) with mechanical and optoelectronic properties across alloy and semiconductor material systems.

Advanced Alloy Development

Characterizing the mechanical performance of multi-component alloy systems (Al-Sn-Cu) through experimental microstructural analysis and property-structure correlations.

Scalable Photovoltaic Manufacturing

Drawing on industrial production engineering experience to address the gap between laboratory-optimized solar cell performance and scalable, cost-effective manufacturing processes.

Future Research Direction

My near-term research goal is to advance the long-term operational stability of perovskite solar cells — the critical bottleneck preventing commercial deployment. I aim to combine computational screening of novel perovskite compositions with experimental fabrication and characterization, ultimately contributing to devices that are both highly efficient and durable under real-world operating conditions. My industrial background in materials processing adds a manufacturing-aware perspective that I believe is essential for translating laboratory breakthroughs into scalable energy solutions.

03 — Projects & Technical Work

Projects & Technical Work

Research Projects

Performance Evaluation of Nanoparticle-Based Interlayered Perovskite Solar Cell
BSc Thesis
Apr 2021 – Mar 2022  ·  KUET, Khulna

Conducted a numerical investigation into efficiency enhancement of perovskite solar cells using nanoparticle-based interlayer engineering. Developed a simulation model in SCAPS-1D incorporating silver nanoparticles as an interlayer, systematically varying material and structural parameters to map their influence on photovoltaic performance metrics.

▶ Achieved a simulated Power Conversion Efficiency (PCE) of 22% — a significant benchmark for nanoparticle-assisted perovskite architectures.

SCAPS-1D Perovskite PV Silver Nanoparticles PCE Optimization Numerical Simulation
Machine Learning-Assisted Perovskite Solar Cell Optimization
Research
Apr 2024 – Apr 2025  ·  Team Research Project

Applied supervised machine learning algorithms to simulate multilayered perovskite solar cell configurations, training models on large parameter datasets generated from device physics simulations. The ML framework identified non-linear relationships between structural parameters and efficiency metrics that conventional simulation sweeps would miss.

▶ Results submitted for peer review. Demonstrated ML as a viable high-throughput screening tool for perovskite device optimization.

Machine Learning SCAPS-1D Python Multilayer Devices Parameter Optimization
First-Principles Analysis of Perovskite Optoelectronic Properties
Research
Jan 2025 – Jun 2025  ·  Collaborative Study

Performed DFT-based first-principles calculations to probe the electronic band structure, optical absorption spectra, and carrier effective masses of candidate perovskite compounds. Insights from ab initio calculations were fed into a device simulation framework to close the loop between atomistic properties and macroscopic performance.

▶ Manuscript in preparation / under review. Establishes a multiscale modeling workflow bridging DFT and device simulation.

Professional / Industry Projects

Process Optimization of 58-Ton Induction Furnace Operations
Industry
Jul 2023 – Present  ·  Bangladesh Steel Re-Rolling Mills Ltd., Chattogram

Led systematic process optimization of a 58-ton induction furnace producing international-grade steel. Analyzed tap-to-tap cycle data to identify bottlenecks, implemented revised charge sequencing and temperature control protocols, and optimized ferroalloy addition schedules based on melt chemistry targets.

▶ Reduced tap-to-tap time by 2 minutes (8% production efficiency gain) and reduced ferroalloy consumption from 13 kg/ton to 10.5 kg/ton, yielding annual savings of approximately 3–4 crores BDT.

Induction Furnace Process Optimization Steel Metallurgy Quality Control Cost Reduction

Industry

05 — About & Background

About & Background

I am a Materials Science and Engineering graduate from Khulna University of Engineering and Technology (KUET), Bangladesh, where I ranked 16th in my cohort of 65 students. My academic journey was defined by a growing fascination with the materials physics underlying next-generation solar energy technologies — a thread that runs from my undergraduate thesis through my current research manuscripts.

Since graduating, I have worked as a Production Engineer at Bangladesh Steel Re-Rolling Mills Limited in Chattogram, operating and optimizing a 58-ton induction furnace in an industrial steel melting environment. While this sits outside the solar cell domain, it has given me an irreplaceable understanding of how materials behave under real process constraints — knowledge that I believe is essential for any researcher serious about translating lab-scale breakthroughs into manufacturable technologies.

In parallel with my industry role, I have continued active research: co-authoring a published study on Al-Sn-Cu alloy microstructure, leading a machine learning-assisted perovskite optimization study, and contributing to a first-principles optoelectronic characterization project. Three manuscripts from this work are currently under review.

I am now seeking PhD or thesis-based Masters positions in photovoltaics, computational materials science, or related fields — particularly in North America and Europe — where I can pursue the stability and scalability challenges that I believe will define the next decade of perovskite solar cell research.
